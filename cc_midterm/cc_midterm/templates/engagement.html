Customer Engagement

In this section of the webpage, we will try to analyze the data and address the question:

How does customer engagement change over time?
For ease of analysis, as well as for statistical best practice, this was done by taking a sample of households. The chosen households are mostly random, but were slightly curated to ensure variance in background and abundance in purchasing activity.

The resulting chosen households can be tagged by their HSHD (ID) values: {{ hshd_vals|join:", " }}.

Total Monthly Expense By House

Here, we can see the total monthly transaction data for each household.

Seeing this data, it's clear theres a wide variety of things happening in each household. First, we can gather that sales are not severely decreasing over time, as it appears that there is always some sort of activity happening regardless of the time of year. However, we also see that purchase activity gets more less abundant and more consistent following January 2021. This is clearly a result of the ongoing pandemic, perhaps implying that it has forced households into more normalized schedules as things like travel calm down, as well as spend less as the US economy begins to decline.
