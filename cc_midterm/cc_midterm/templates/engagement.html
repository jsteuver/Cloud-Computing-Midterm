Customer Engagement

In this section of the webpage, we will try to analyze the data and address the question:

How does customer engagement change over time?
We can first look at this by looking at overall purchases across all households...

Total Monthly Sales

{% include "line_chart_labeled.html" with props=all_data_props only %}

Here, it's clear that in general, purchases have been fairly steady until March 2020 where wee a sudden spike in sales. This is likely due to the COVID19 pandemic, as this was when it first began to hit the US, causing people to horde supplies. After this point, we can see it begins to decline, but still remains relatively higher than the average sales of previous months.

For ease of analysis, as well as for statistical best practice, we can dive further by taking a sample of households. The chosen households are mostly random, but were slightly curated to ensure variance in background and abundance in purchasing activity.

The resulting chosen households can be tagged by their HSHD (ID) values: {{ hshd_vals|join:", " }}, which we can then analyze similarly...

Total Monthly Expense By House

{% include "line_chart_labeled.html" with props=per_house_props %}

Here, we can see the total monthly transaction data for each household.

Seeing this data, it's clear theres a wide variety of things happening in each household. First, we can gather that sales are not severely decreasing over time, as it appears that there is always some sort of activity happening regardless of the time of year. However, we also see that purchase activity in these households gets less abundant and more consistent following January 2020. This is clearly a result of the ongoing pandemic, and we can even see a slight spike across most of the households around March 2020, as we've also seen in the overall income. This perhaps implies that the pandemic has forced households into more normalized schedules as things like travel calmed down, as well resulting in less spending as the US economy began its initial decline.
